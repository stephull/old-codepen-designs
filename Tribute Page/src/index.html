Ryuichi Sakamoto

坂本 龍一



About

Ryuichi Sakamoto is a Japanese composer, pianist, songwriter, record producer, and actor born in Japan.

Beginning his career in 1970, Sakamoto was enrolled in the Tokyo National University of Fine Arts and Music to pursue a Bachelor of Arts in music composition. He would later earn his Master of Arts in specializing electronic and ethnic music. Sakamoto would study various types of world music, such as those mostly identifying from African, Indian, and native Okinawan traditions. He also experimented with different types of electronic music, to which he eventually partnered with musicians Haruomi Hosono and Yukihiro Takahashi to create the now famous band, Yellow Magic Orchestra, also known as YMO.


Yellow Magic Orchestra's first titular album was met with critical acclaim when released in 1979. Some of the most popular singles to be released from the album were songwritten by Sakamoto himself. Experimenting with genres such as synthpop, electronica, ambient music, techno, and more, the group influenced a new wave of electronic music, some of which has influenced other artists outside of Japan. Sakamoto would release his first solo album called "Thousand Knives of Ryuichi Sakamoto". Like "Yellow Magic Orchestra", the album comprised of songs experimenting with different electronic music instruments and was also met with success.
